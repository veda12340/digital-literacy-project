case Study: UPI Payment Fraud (The "Refund" Scam) What it is: UPI payment fraud occurs when malicious actors manipulate victims into authorizing direct money transfers from their bank accounts using Unified Payments Interface (UPI) applications (such as Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm), often under the deceptive guise of sending the victim a refund, cashback, or prize.

How it happens (Step-by-Step):

Initial Contact: The scammer calls or messages the victim, posing as a customer service representative from a trusted company, bank, or online marketplace, claiming the victim is owed a refund.

Building Trust: The scammer uses a professional tone and may recite basic, publicly available information (like the victim's name or phone number) to appear legitimate.

The Hook: The scammer sends a "payment request" link or a QR code to the victim's smartphone.

The Trap: The scammer instructs the victim to open their UPI app, click the link, enter their secret UPI PIN, and hit "Proceed." They falsely claim that entering the PIN is required to receive the money into their account.

The Theft: Because a UPI PIN is only ever used to send money, entering it instantly deducts funds from the victim’s account and transfers them to the scammer.

Who is targeted: Scammers frequently target college students, elderly individuals, and users buying or selling goods on second-hand platforms. They prey on a lack of digital financial literacy and the psychological appeal of unexpected money.

Consequences: The primary consequence is immediate, often unrecoverable financial loss due to the instantaneous nature of UPI transfers. Victims also experience severe emotional distress, anxiety, and a lingering loss of trust in digital banking systems.
